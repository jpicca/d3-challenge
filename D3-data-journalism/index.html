Correlations Demographics and Health Trends

It's fairly evident that states with lower median incomes tend to see higher proportions of obese residents. One potential cause is that the lower nutritional value of cheaper foods could lead to higher occurrences of obesity among a population. Additionally, reduced access to healthcare and fitness programs likely promotes higher rates of obesity.

Another interesting trend in our data is the correlation between the smoking rate and median income / % in poverty. In states with lower incomes and higher poverty rates, smoking prevalence appears to be higher. One possible influence again related to healthcare / wellness access. With reduced income, addictions such as smoking may be tougher to overcome.

Regarding median age, younger states appear to have a greater percentage of individuals without healthcare. This could be an artifact of younger individuals generally being less incentivized to purchase healthcare, due to their lower personal wealth and better background health (versus older individuals).
